Methods Summary

We will work with two nonprofit organizations: Casa Latina, in Seattle, WA, and El Comedor, in Nogales, Mexico. Members and beneficiaries of these organizations will be invited to participate in the project, which consists of 1) participatory photography, and 2) life stories elicited through the photos. Work in Nogales explores the experience of the immigrants at the turning point of their migration process, the transience of a shelter on the side of the US-Mexico border. Work in Seattle explores the experience of the immigrants as they settle in their (generally precarious) existence as day laborers. A future phase of the project will explore the experience of migrants as they return to their place of origin, either voluntarily (choice of return) or involuntarily (deported), in a village in southern Mexico or northern Guatemala

  1. Participatory Photography: participants in each site will be lent a digital camera and asked to take pictures of their daily life activities (we will experiment with three different types of prompts: broad, specific, list).

  2. Life Stories: we will hold semi-structured debriefing interviews to discuss images they bring back. Interviews will be transcribed and analyzed, together with the images, for recurring themes and salient topics.
